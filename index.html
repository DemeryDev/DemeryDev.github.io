Truned Cases

With this issue, we were getting a lot of turned cases. I came up with a solution to fabricate center rails and a gate. I drew everything in AutoCAD and then order the parts. The gate is mounted to an air cylinder that is programmed to the conveyor PLC. Using a few photo eye sensors I can count the cases on each side and stop one side to let the other side startup. Also, I am using two more cylinders one on each side which acts as a brake to stop cases from moving.

Cases Lifting Up

We were getting an extra case lifting due to backpressure. I came up with the idea to add wheels to stop the cases from lifting. With this idea, I drew everything in AutoCAD and created draws to have the side pieces fabricated by a machine shop. Order the other parts then put everything together and install. Now all the operator has to do is remove during the size while not running that size.

Part Wearing Out Too Fast

The machine parts were wearing out way too fast. I designed a new block that holds a bearing for the shaft/suction cup to slide through. When this update I had to update the shaft to a harder material so the bearing would not ware the shaft.

Part Design

This was a simple design. There was dead plates causing too much drag. Using autoCAD I designed dead plates which were made by a machine shop. Wants they were made I install small bearings so the cases would just ride over top of the plates leaving behind the drag.

Going Tray-less

With the tray-less project, I was asked to send 4 cartons through our packer to make one case without cardboard. Saving tons of money in cardboard savings. To do this I added a red belt to make a total of 4 belt so the cartons would have support. Then added a metal center guide so after the cartons were transfer to the next chain they would have support as well. Also as you can see in the picture my new dead plate design was useed in the packer.

The Rejection System

After applying two stickers on a case there was no way of knowing they were on. I came up with a design and drew it out then ordered parts for the project. I installed a few photo eye sensors and two banner cameras that would take pictures of the front and rear of each case. Then installed an air cylinder with a paddle on the end and a conveyor mounted to the side so the cases have somewhere to go. Everything is programmed through the conveyor PLC. I also ordered and install the safety fencing with one gate that was programmed with a safety relay through the PLC. If the door was open the air cylinder becomes disable and the conveyors stop.

To help The Tanker Drivers

This is a screen I made to help the sugar tanker drivers to get feed back from our sugar system when they are off loading dry sugar. This screen gets feed back from the sugar system PLC and displays live data on the screen.

VNC Viewer

This program allows you to view HMI screens with one click. You can add, edit and remove different HMIs. Just need to add a Name, IP and if needed a password. Then you just select the name and click connect. The program opens VNC viewer that is installed on the computer to the correct IP.

Invertor Program

This is an inventory program for a stockroom. It allows a user to scan a series of bar codes that will get uploaded to SQL. It also does matter what order you scan these barcodes in because I wrote the program to move the scanned item into the correct textbox. The first barcode would be a person's name. Next is a part number. Then using a sheet with preprinted barcodes with the different quantities you scan just one barcode with the right quantity. After all three textboxes are filled this information goes to the SQL to be stored. If the user only fills one or two of the textboxes after one minute whatever that is in the textboxes gets sent to SQL and the textboxes clear and wait for the next entry. Once every 24 hours data is pulled from SQL and is emailed out. This program is fully automatic and the user doesn't need to do anything but scan with the bar coder scanner.
